#ManyDesignsCarbon

A crowd many-designs project!

About the project

with #ManyDesignsCarbon we are running a crowd-sourced project in fighting climate change!

In research teams (RTs) of up to two, everyone – from PhD student to full professor from around the world – will have the possibility to propose an experimental research design, all assessing the same research question:

What is the impact of behavioral interventions on real-world support for a social price of carbon?

The project coordinators (PCs) will use the results of #ManyDesignsCarbon to learn about the scientific process and which behavioral intervention is most promising in enhancing support for the social price of carbon. They will study the variation in design choices and results, and write a metascience paper on the topic where all contributors whose design proposals were run will also be included as co-authors.

Procedure

With successful registration, there are six stages in #ManyDesignsCarbon: registration, submission of design proposals via a pre-registration, peer assessment, implementation of the experiment, data analysis, and writing of the paper.

  1. Registration
    1. Research teams (RTs) sign up for taking part in this study (without yet submitting a research design).
    2. Project coordinators (PCs) pre-screen applications for adherence with the eligibility conditions and invite all eligible RTs to submit a pre-registration outlining their design proposal.

  2. Design submission and selection procedure
    1. Invited RTs submit a pre-registration outlining their research proposal. Additionally, RTs will fill out a survey asking RTs about their beliefs about standardized effect sizes of their design (Cohen’s d or Cohen’s h) the meta-analytical effect size across all designs, and their belief of design heterogeneity.
    2. PCs pre-screen all submitted designs for adherence with the design conditions.
    3. If more than 42 design proposals are eligible, 42 designs will be randomly selected for being included in the study.

  3. Peer assessment
    4. RTs will assess 10 other RT's design proposals. Specifically, RTs will be asked to assess:
    • how informative the proposed research design is on a Likert scale from 0 (not at all informative) to 10 (very informative) with the following question: "What is the impact of behavioral interventions on real-world support for a social price of carbon?"
    • under which type of behavioral intervention a project using this design would be classified (open endend).
    • the standardized effect size of the design (Cohen's d or Cohen's h depending on how the dependent veriable of the design is constructed).

  4. Experimental implementation
    • All selected designs will be implemented as online experiments following this experimental protocol using Prolific, payed for by the PCs.
    • All selected RTs program the experimental software for their design proposal themselves.
    • All selected RTs host the experimental software for their design proposal on their own servers.

  5. Analysis
    • RTs will submit their raw data and give PCs access to the server on which the experiment is hosted.
    • PCs will analyze the data as specified in RTs' respective pre-registrations.
    • PCs will analyze the data as specified in the PCs' pre-analysis plan.

  6. PCs will write a metascience paper; all members of selected RTs will be co-authors.

Project coordinators. The project will be coordinated by researchers from the University of Innsbruck. Meet the project coordinators.

Pre-analysis plan. Before inviting RTs to the study a pre-analysis plan (PAP) was uploaded outlining the design of the study. This frozen "pre-analysis plan" was uploaded to the Open Science Framework on May 13, 2024. Once all RTs have submitted their pre-registrations of their designs, but before the data collection starts, we will specify a standardized way to analyze the data and the exact hypotheses and tests as an addendum to the initial PAP.

Questions. We have set up a detailed FAQ with commonly asked question. Of course, feel free to contact us via mail.

Why participate?

  • In addition to being part of a fascinating project, you will get the chance to satisfy the curiosity of the academic in you, hungry for answers to a highly relevant research question.
  • For the proposals selected for implementation, the project coordinators will cover the costs for participant payments.
  • If your proposal is selected for implementation, you will become co-author on the paper that targets publication in a top scientific journal. Two of the PCs previously organized a similar many-designs project on "Competition and Moral behavior", published in PNAS in 2023 (see Huber et al., 2023).

Eligibility

To be eligible for taking part this project, research teams (RTs) have to follow the following eligibility conditions:

  • maximum of two people per RT
  • at least one team member needs a PhD in Economics, Psychology or a related field
  • at least one team member needs one experimental study that is either published (accepted for publication) or a working paper
  • RTs have to briefly justify why they consider themselves eligible for the study
  • each researcher can only be a member of one RT

Borderline criteria will be voted on by the project coordinators (majority rule).

Experimental protocol

In general the experiment follows the protocol shown in the illustration above - parts of experiment shown in white are provided by the project coordinators (PCs); green parts are submitted by selected research teams (RTs) in their proposals. In total the experiment is estimated to last 15 minutes, where 7-8 minutes are attributed to the intervention.

INTRO: Participants will go through an general introduction of the experiment including a given consent and a CAPTCHA task.

INTERVENTION: After the general introduction participants are randomly forwarded to one of two treatment conditions (i.e., either CONTROL or POLICY) of the 42 selected designs, where also the measure for real-world support for the social price of carbon (also proposed by the RTs in their desing) is elicited. The proposed design by the RTs have to adhere to the design conditions.

EX-POST QUESTIONS: Following that, participants fill out an ex-post questionnaire provided by the PCs (RTs get the otree app or qualtrics instance to incorporate the ex-post questionnaire into their software from the PCs). The particular questions included can be seen in the pre-analysis plan on Open Science Framework.

END: Having answered the ex-post questionnaire, participants are reached the end of the experiment and will get their payout (i.e., flat fee of 1.50 GBP plus a potential bonus of max. 1.50 GBP; the latter is up to the RTs).

Design conditions

To be included in this project, the submitted pre-registration has to adhere to the following design conditions:

  • Design is implemented as an online experiment to be run on Prolific with 350 observations:
    • The experiment needs to be administered online on Prolific.
    • Design adheres to Prolific's terms & conditions for researchers.
    • The intervention should not exceed 7-8 minutes.
  • Employ a between-subjects treatment design with two equally sized treatments/conditions:
    • One control condition without intervention.
    • One treatment condition with intervention.
    • RTs should be able to provide PCs with two links, one for each condition (either two links on otree or qualtrics, depending on which software is used).
  • Incentive compatible payments for subjects that cover at least the opportunity cost of time (Prolific implements the requirement of GBP 5 per hour as minimum payment), and are not negative (losses).
  • Define a clear measure of real-world support for the social price of carbon.
  • No deception of subjects.
  • Subject anonymity regarding who is interacting with whom during the experiment.
  • No measurements of physical state (e.g., saliva samples, blood samples) and no physical or psychological harm.
  • Clear information for subjects regarding the experiment’s duration, repetitions, interactions, and random processes (like lotteries) that are relevant for subjects, and which information is common knowledge to other (groups of) subjects.
  • Design clearly defines randomization procedures across treatments.

All submitted proposals will be pre-screened by the project coordinators (PCs) to eliminate studies that do not fit the above-mentioned criteria. Borderline criteria will be voted on by the project coordinators (majority rule).
