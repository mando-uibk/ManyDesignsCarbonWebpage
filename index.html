#ManyDesignsCarbon

A Crowd-Science Project

About the project

#ManyDesignsCarbon is an innovative crowd-science initiative focused on exploring the influence of behavioral interventions on societal support for carbon pricing.

This project unites research teams from across the globe, ranging from PhD students to tenured professors, to develop and propose experimental designs. Each team of up to two researchers will address a critical question:

What is the impact of behavioral interventions on real-world support for a social price of carbon?

Project coordinators will analyze the outcomes to identify the most effective strategies for enhancing public support for carbon pricing. This exploration will culminate in a comprehensive metascience paper, co-authored by all participants whose designs were implemented. The study will highlight variations in design choices and their results, contributing valuable insights into the scientific process and the potential of behavioral science to address environmental challenges.

Procedure

Participation in #ManyDesignsCarbon involves six key stages: Sign-up, Design Proposal Submission, Filling out two Surveys (A and B), Programming of the Experiment, and Manuscript Writing (optional).

  1. Registration
    1. Research teams (RTs) register to participate without initially submitting a research design.
    2. Project coordinators (PCs) then screen these applications for eligibility according to the project's eligibility criteria and invite compliant RTs to outline their design proposals through a pre-registration.

  2. Design submission and selection procedure
    1. Eligible RTs submit a detailed pre-registration of their research proposals and complete Survey A on their design's expected effect size (Cohen’s d or Cohen’s h), the anticipated meta-analytical effect size across all designs, and design heterogeneity.
    2. PCs pre-screen all submitted designs for adherence with the design conditions.
    3. If eligible submissions exceed 42, a random selection will be made to choose 42 designs for inclusion in the study.

  3. Survey B
    4. RTs are required to evaluate ten other design proposals. Specifically, for each the designs, RTs will be asked
    • how informative the proposed research design is to answer the following research question (Likert scale from 0 to 10): "What is the impact of behavioral interventions on real-world support for a social price of carbon?"
    • to classify the type of behavioral intervention employed (open endend).
    • predict the standardized effect size of the design (Cohen's d or Cohen's h depending on the dependent variable).

  4. Experimental implementation
    • Selected designs are implemented as online experiments on Prolific following this experimental protocol, and funded by the PCs (i.e., 350 observations per implemented design).
    • RTs are responsible for programming and hosting the experimental software (including instructions) on their servers, ensuring 350 observations per design.

  5. Analysis
    • RTs must specify that main treatment effects can be estimated using Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) or a logit model, with the dependent variable being support for a social price of carbon.
    • Upon completion, PCs download the raw data to which they must be granted acces by the RTs, who also need to provide a detailed codebook of all data points.
    • PCs will analyze the data as specified in the PCs' pre-analysis plan.

  6. PCs will write a metascience paper; all members of selected RTs will be co-authors.

Project coordinators. The project will be coordinated by researchers from the University of Innsbruck. Meet the project coordinators.

Pre-analysis plan. A detailed Pre-Analysis Plan (PAP) was registered with the Open Science Framework on May 15, 2024. An addendum specifying standardized analysis methods and exact hypotheses will follow after design registration but before data collection commences.

Questions. We have set up a detailed FAQ with commonly asked question. Of course, feel free to contact us via mail.

Why participate?

  • Joining #ManyDesignsCarbon offers more than just engagement in a groundbreaking project. It satisfies your academic curiosity with the opportunity to explore a significant and timely research question.
  • For designs that are chosen for implementation, the project coordinators will fund participant payments, alleviating financial concerns and allowing you to focus on research excellence.
  • If your design is selected, you will gain co-authorship on a paper slated for submission to a leading scientific journal. This follows in the footsteps of previous successful project by two of our PCs on "Competition and Moral Behavior", published in PNAS in 2023 (refer to Huber et al., 2023).)

Eligibility

To participate in this project, research teams (RTs) must meet the following eligibility criteria:

  • each RT may consist of no more than two members.
  • at least one team member must hold a PhD in Economics, Psychology, or a related field.
  • at least one team member must have conducted an experimental study that is published, accepted for publication, or documented as a working paper.
  • RTs are required to submit a brief justification of their eligibility for the project.
  • each researcher may only participate in one RT.

Cases that meet borderline criteria will be decided through a majority vote by the project coordinators.

Experimental protocol

The protocol for our experiment is outlined in the diagram above. Components in white are managed by the Project Coordinators (PCs), while the green parts represent the contributions from selected Research Teams (RTs). The entire experiment is designed to take approximately 15 minutes, with 7-8 minutes dedicated to the intervention.

INTRO: Participants begin with a comprehensive introduction that includes consent procedures and a CAPTCHA task to ensure genuine responses by preventing bots.

INTERVENTION: Following the introduction, participants are randomly assigned to one of two conditions—CONTROL or POLICY—across the 42 selected designs. This phase includes the measurement of real-world support for a social price on carbon, as outlined by the RTs in their proposals. All designs must meet the specified design conditions.

EX-POST QUESTIONS: Participants then complete a follow-up questionnaire provided by the PCs. RTs integrate this questionnaire into their software (either oTree or Qualtrics), which will be provided by the PCs. Specific questions are detailed in our pre-analysis plan available on the Open Science Framework.

END: Having answered the ex-post questionnaire, participants are at the end of the experiment and will get their payout (i.e., flat fee of 1.50 GBP plus a potential bonus of max. 3 GBP on average; the latter is up to the RTs).

Design conditions

To be included in this project, the submitted pre-registration has to adhere to the following design conditions:

  • Each design is implemented as an online experiment to be run on Prolific with 350 observations
    • The experiment needs to be suitable for administration on Prolific.
    • Designs must adhere to Prolific's terms & conditions for researchers.
    • Interventions should not exceed 7-8 minutes, inclusive of dependent variable elicitation.
  • Experiments must employ a between-subjects design with two conditions:
    • A control condition without intervention (CONTROL).
    • A treatment condition with intervention (POLICY).
    • RTs must ensure the possibility to generate two distinct links for each condition on either oTree or Qualtrics (PCs will be granted access to the experiments to generate the links).
  • RTs need to ensure incentive compatible payments that should at least cover the participants' opportunity costs and adhere to Prolific’s minimum payment guideline of GBP 6 per hour without imposing negative earnings.
  • RTs must define a clear metric for real-world support for the social price of carbon.
  • RTs must avoid deception of subjects.
  • RTs must ensure subject anonymity within the experiment
  • No measurements of physical state (e.g., saliva samples, blood samples) and no physical or psychological harm.
  • RTs need to provide clear information on experiment duration, repetitions, interactions, and randomization procedures.

All proposals are subject to an initial review by the PCs to ensure compliance with these standards. Proposals meeting borderline criteria will be decided through a majority vote by the PCs.
